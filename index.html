Year 2 (82%)

Module Breakdown:

  • Digital Design with HDL - 96% - An introduction to digital design concepts along with the appropriate role of Hardware Description Languages (HDLs) in the modern digital design flow. Particular emphasis on using HDLs for the synthesis of digital circuits on one side and on the development of appropriate testing through the use of HDL test benches and simulation.
  • Algorithms & Numerical Methods - 81% - An introduction to software design and implementation, providing solid grounding in the theory and applications of data structures and algorithms. The module also covers the mathematical theory of numerical methods and their applications.
  • Engineering Mathematics, Signals & Systems - 80% - An inroduction to more advanced mathematical tools that are useful for modelling real-world engineering systems and for the analysis and processing of signals (Multivariable Tools, Laplace transform and Fourier Transform).
  • Noise, Waves & Fields - 83% - Provided essential content for the design of analogue and digital circuits, by considering the effects of noise and interference on circuit performance along with the basic concepts of electromagnetic compatibility. It introduced transmission line effects on interconnects such as delay, reflections, and crosstalk, which are important considerations in any high speed digital circuit and in transmission of signals over cables. The electromagnetic theory of fields and waves is analysed in order to underpin the theory of transmission lines and provide the foundations for later courses.
  • Semiconductor Devices & Circuits - 86% - An introduction to the most heavily used semiconductor devices (diode, bipolar junction transistor and field effect transistor) in electronic engineering with an emphasis on the analysis and design of common building blocks in analogue and digital electronics.
  • Design, Construction & Test - 79% - Work in a group to develop a practical electronic product (a multimeter) using analogue design and an embedded ARM processor.
  • Java Programming - 70% - Taught basic skills in Java programming and how to use those within software engineering to produce a significant application.

Year 1 (88%)

Module Breakdown:

  • Mathematics - 95% - An introduction to a range of fundamental mathematical tools for the design, modelling and analysis of engineering systems and signals.
  • Analogue Electronics & Physics - 89% - An introduction to the basic circuit elements (resistors, capacitors, inductors, diodes and operational amplifiers), explaing the physics behind their operation, and the techniques used to design and analyse circuits built using them.
  • Digital Circuits - 96% - An introduction to the operation of digital logic circuits in a variety of applications, and tools and techniques for their design and analysis.
  • Engineering Design - 71% - A group project to develop skills and knowledge in team working, analysis, design, problem solving and idea creation by creating a game using a BBC Microbit.
  • Introduction to Programming - 89% - An introduction to programming, teaching Python and C and creating a project at the end of both courses.
  • Digital Systems - 78% - An introduction to the inner workings of modern high-performance computing systems, from arithmetic logic functions via microprocessors to multi-processor systems.
